# LinkedIn Campaign — Post 5: Innovation Highlights

**Type**: Innovation showcase
**Tone**: Substantive, specific, evidence-linked
**Target audience**: CTOs, CSOs, innovation officers, technology strategists

---

The AnubisX Framework introduces several innovations in the science of digital attribution. Each is documented in the repository with formal definitions and cross-references.

**1. Cognitive Centroid Theory**

The first formalization of behavioral identity as an asymptotic attractor in feature space. The Cognitive Centroid is defined as the expected value of an individual's behavioral feature distribution: θ_i = E[ **X** | i ]. Estimated by: θ̂_n = (1/n) Σ y_i. Convergence is governed by HYP-CORE-005.

**Status**: Internal theoretical construct. Empirically testable through designed experiments (EXP-CP-001—005). **Source**: Theory/Core_Theory.md (THY-001), Theory/Identity_Attribution_Theory.md (THY-004)

**2. Involuntary Signal Hierarchy**

A formal ranking of behavioral features by resistance to counter-forensic manipulation (HYP-BEH-005). Core involuntary signals (function-word frequencies, inter-event timing distributions, error recovery patterns) are theoretically resistant to conscious modification. The hierarchy has 4 levels, from core involuntary (highest resistance) to preference-based (lowest resistance).

**Status**: Theoretical proposition requiring empirical validation. **Source**: Axioms/Behavior_Axioms.md (HYP-BEH-005), Theory/Theory_of_Digital_Cognitive_Fingerprints.md (THY-002)

**3. Multi-Modal Fusion Comparison**

Three fusion strategies are specified within a single architecture — score-level (ALG-030), feature-level via HOSVD (ALG-031, PROPOSED), and decision-level via Dempster-Shafer (ALG-032) — enabling future empirical comparison of fusion effectiveness for behavioral data.

**Status**: Specified but not empirically compared. **Source**: Algorithms/Fusion_Algorithms.md (ALG-029—032)

**4. Systematic Error Decomposition**

Total error is decomposed into five components: measurement, context, estimation, temporal, and method (VLD-006). Six false-positive categories (FP-001—006) and six false-negative categories (FN-001—006) are specified with root cause analysis protocols.

**Status**: Design specification. Protocols pending empirical validation. **Source**: Validation/Error_Analysis.md (VLD-006), Validation/False_Positive_Analysis.md (VLD-007), Validation/False_Negative_Analysis.md (VLD-008)

**5. Four-Tier Validation with Quantified Criteria**

31 acceptance thresholds across four tiers — from unit-level numerical stability (variance < 1e-6) to system-level operational performance (AUC ≥ 0.95, FAR drift < 0.1%/month). All thresholds were specified before implementation.

**Status**: Provisional design targets pending empirical calibration. **Source**: Validation/Acceptance_Criteria.md (VLD-010)

**6. Complete Algorithm Catalog**

37 algorithm specifications with full traceability to axioms, equations, and validation criteria. Each specifies inputs, outputs, mathematical functions, dependencies, and acceptance criteria.

**Status**: Specified but not implemented (33 at ESTABLISHED, 2 PROPOSED, 2 TBD, 1 FUTURE_RESEARCH). **Source**: Algorithms/Algorithm_Catalog.md

**What these innovations share**: They are theoretical contributions. They are fully specified but untested empirically. The next phase is implementation and validation.
