## Scientific Foundations

The AnubisX Framework builds upon decades of research across multiple disciplines. We gratefully acknowledge the scientific communities whose work provides the foundation for this framework.

### Stylometry and Authorship Analysis

- The computational stylometry community for foundational methods in authorship attribution
- Researchers in function-word analysis and syntactic stylometry
- Cross-platform authorship attribution studies

### Forensic Science

- The forensic statistics community for the Likelihood Ratio framework
- Developers of the Daubert standard and forensic science methodology
- The fingerprint, DNA, and forensic document examination communities for standards in error rate quantification

### Cognitive Science and Psycholinguistics

- Researchers in cognitive processing habits and procedural memory
- Studies in linguistic idiolect and individual language variation
- Work on attentional habits and cognitive load

### Behavioral Biometrics

- The keystroke dynamics and mouse dynamics research communities
- Gait analysis and behavioral pattern recognition researchers
- Multi-modal biometric fusion research

### Signal Detection and Decision Theory

- The statistical decision theory and ROC analysis communities
- Calibration research (PAV, logistic regression, empirical cross-entropy)

## Prototype Validation Data

We gratefully acknowledge the **31 Egyptian Twitter users** whose publicly available tweet data was used for the prototype validation experiments. Their public contributions to social media discourse made it possible to empirically test the framework's stylometric attribution methodology and demonstrate the feasibility of behavioral identity attribution at scale.

## Open-Source Libraries

The Anubis Twitter v2.5 prototype relies on the following open-source libraries:

- **FAISS** (Facebook AI Research) — efficient similarity search and dense vector clustering
- **Transformers** (Hugging Face) — tokenization and text preprocessing infrastructure
- **NumPy** — numerical computing and feature vector operations
- **scikit-learn** — normalization pipeline and evaluation metrics
- **pandas** — data management and experimental results analysis
- **Matplotlib** / **Seaborn** — visualization and figure generation for experimental reports
- **Jupyter** — interactive development and reproducible experimental notebooks
- **tqdm** — progress tracking for batch processing
