## The Challenge

Digital attribution — determining the human operator behind online activity — relies on transient technical indicators that sophisticated adversaries can easily change. When IP addresses are routed through VPNs, device fingerprints are randomized, and network artifacts are scrubbed, traditional attribution fails.

## The Paradigm Shift

The AnubisX Framework introduces a fundamental shift: **from tracking what adversaries use to tracking who adversaries are**. Every individual possesses involuntary cognitive signatures — patterns of language, timing, interaction, social structure, and environmental organization — that are:

- **Unique** to each individual
- **Persistent** across platforms and technical environments
- **Resistant** to conscious modification
- **Measurable** through structured observation

## The Framework

Built on 31 formal axioms, the framework defines a complete scientific methodology for behavioral identity attribution:

- **Five tracking modalities**: Stylometry, chrono-profiling, terminal profiling, network analysis, media forensics
- **Multi-modal fusion**: Combining independent signals via tensor mathematics and evidence theory
- **Quantified uncertainty**: Attribution strength expressed as Likelihood Ratios
- **Daubert-compliant**: Designed for admissibility in legal proceedings

## Current Status

The AnubisX Framework has advanced from theoretical specification to validated prototype. The Anubis Twitter Intelligence Prototype v2.5 has been implemented and tested with 15 executed experiments on 31 Egyptian Twitter accounts. Of 10 core scientific claims, 6 are confirmed publication-ready, with the remainder blocked by data availability or model authentication dependencies.
