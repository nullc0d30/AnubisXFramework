## 1. Cognitive Science Foundations

### 1.1 Individual Differences in Cognitive Processing

Cognitive psychology has established that individuals differ systematically in:
- **Attention**: Allocation, focus duration, distractibility
- **Memory**: Encoding, storage, retrieval patterns
- **Language**: Processing speed, vocabulary, syntactic preferences
- **Motor control**: Timing, coordination, sequencing
- **Executive function**: Planning, monitoring, adjusting

These differences manifest in observable behavioral patterns.

### 1.2 Procedural Memory and Automaticity

Repeated cognitive and motor actions become proceduralized — executed automatically without conscious attention. Proceduralized behaviors are:
- Highly stable over time
- Resistant to interference
- Difficult to consciously modify
- Individually distinctive

## 2. Linguistic Foundations

### 2.1 Stylometry

Stylometric analysis measures linguistic style — the "how" rather than the "what" of communication. Key findings:
- Function-word frequencies are stable individual markers
- Syntactic preferences persist across topics
- Vocabulary richness varies systematically between individuals
- Readability and complexity metrics are individually distinctive

### 2.2 Psycholinguistic Stability

Research shows that core linguistic patterns remain stable over years and across communicative contexts.

## 3. Statistical Foundations

### 3.1 Likelihood Ratio Framework

The LR framework is the established standard for forensic evidence evaluation, used in DNA analysis, fingerprint comparison, and other forensic disciplines.

### 3.2 Multi-Modal Fusion

Information fusion across independent modalities improves accuracy when modality-specific errors are uncorrelated.
