## 1. Theoretical Innovations

### 1.1 Cognitive Centroid Theory

The first formalization of behavioral identity as an asymptotic attractor in feature space. The Cognitive Centroid provides a mathematically rigorous foundation for understanding why behavioral patterns converge toward stable points.

### 1.2 Axiomatic Foundation

31 formal axioms establish Identity Intelligence as a scientific discipline with defined boundaries, internal consistency, and testable predictions.

### 1.3 Involuntary Signal Hierarchy

A formal account of which behavioral features are most resistant to conscious modification, providing a theoretical basis for counter-forensic resistance.

## 2. Methodological Innovations

### 2.1 Multi-Modal Behavioral Fusion

The first structured comparison of fusion strategies for five behavioral modalities, including score-level, feature-level, and decision-level approaches.

### 2.2 Four-Tier Validation Hierarchy

A validation framework specific to behavioral attribution systems, with tiered criteria from unit testing through operational deployment.

### 2.3 Cross-Platform Normalization

A method for removing platform-specific bias from behavioral features while preserving individual distinguishing characteristics.

## 3. Practical Innovations

### 3.1 Standardized Behavioral Metrics

The first comprehensive set of evaluation metrics designed specifically for behavioral attribution.

### 3.2 Benchmark Library

24 standardized benchmarks enabling reproducible comparison of behavioral attribution methods.

### 3.3 Daubert-Compliant Design

The framework is designed from inception to meet legal standards for scientific evidence admissibility.
